Global Access of The Internet

Hover over the map for more details
This visualisation focuses on the growth of the most dominant modern telecommunication technologies used in the world today. It explores the global growth of Fixed Broadband, Fixed Telephone and Mobile Cellular Subscriptions.
While these technologies represent the physical mediums that are used to communicate around the world, the internet is the hub that provides a platform for the information to be shared. This map shows how much of the world's population accesses the internet. With the exception of Africa, most nations have more the 50% of their populations accessing the internet.

Growth of Various Telecommunication Technologies

The line chart shows the proliferation of mobile cellular subscriptions over time. Only since the late 1990s, mobile devices have quickly gained huge popularity compared to broadband and telephones. Mobile has even surpassed the global average in 2017. Meaning there are more mobile subscriptions in the world than people.

Relationship between Income and Technology Popularity

Click on the buttons to switch the technology. Interact with the graph and the legend to filter the data points and gain more info.
These plots show the relationships between each of the three technologies and the income per capita for each country (as measured by the Atlas method)
These plots provide some insight into the extraodinary popularity of mobile devices. While the subscription rate (per 100 people) seems to be heavily correlated with the average income of a country for both telephones and broadband, the affordability of cheap smart phones, have them accessible to most people regardless of their economic circumstances. For example, Indonesia is one of the highest countries in terms of use of Mobile phones, while its average income of its population if fairly low.

The Exports and Imports of ICT Goods

Hover over the heat map cells to gain info on the average value of that cell
This heat map helps to communicate the wider implications of implementing modern telecommunications technologies. The linear trend in the heat map indicates a strong correlation between a country's import of ICT services and its exports. This suggests that strong infrastructure in broadband and mobile cellular towers is needed so a country's population can start to access ICT goods and services. This interaction in the online economy can help create jobs and provide an avenue for individuals who previously struggled to find work.
